Affiliated Members

We are lucky enough to have some amazing external members, who we regularly collaborate with.

Mario Giulianelli

Postdoctoral Researcher, ETH Zurich

What I am most excited about is studying human language use via computational models of language understanding and generation. My research interests lie in the following areas, which roughly define the boundaries of my projects and publications: Computational semantics and pragmatics, Neural language and dialogue modelling, Natural language generation, Explainability, interpretability, and evaluation of NLP models, Language variation and change in communities of speakers.

Jaap Jumelet

PhD Student, University of Amsterdam

I am a PhD candidate in the group of Jelle Zuidema at the ILLC, University of Amsterdam. The topic of my PhD lies at the intersection of explainable AI and natural language processing. I'm interested in uncovering the linguistic capacities of current NLP models, and developing new techniques that allow us to gain these insights in a robust and faithful way.

Anastasia Klimovich Gray

Assistant Professor, University of Aberdeen

I am a cognitive neuroscientist and a lecturer at the University of Aberdeen School of Psychology. Together with students and collaborators in our lab we study how cortical networks optimize and adapt language comprehension strategies in different environments and neurodiverse populations. Current research projects focus on the neural basis of adaptive speech processing, effects of spoken language on reading development, dyslexia, and machine learning tools for brain data analysis

Agnieszka Konopka

Assistant Professor, University of Aberdeen

My research addresses questions in language production and memory for language. My research interests include: Language production, message formulation, sentence formulation, incrementality and flexibility in language processing, cross-linguistic differences in language processing, linguistic diversity, bilingualism, language and though, implicit learning, syntax, memory for language, source memory, forgetting, metacognition
